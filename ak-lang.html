Alaska Native Language Revitalization

Here we will discuss the next-steps for Alaska in the journey to revitalize and strengthen Alaska Native language use all across the State as laid out by Dr. X̱ʼunei Lance Twitchell.

Dr. X̱ʼunei Lance Twitchell is a proffesor of linguistics and the Lingít language at the University of Alaska Southeast. X̱ʼunei is also an advocate all across the State of Alaska and the rest of the world for Indigenous language revitalization.

Hear more from X̱ʼunei Lance Twitchell
Step 1. Step 2. Step 3. Step 4.
Establish a college of Alaska Native languages
This involves creating an institution within the University of Alaska that is dedicated to Alaska Native languages and has the ability to fully certify teachers to teach Alaska Native languages. (Twitchell, 2020)
Re-imagine the Board of Education in Alaska
This involves reshaping the Board of Education to be in charge of school districts, charter schools, correspondence schools, and Alaska Native language schools and programs. (Twitchell, 2020)
Establish an Alaska Native school consortium
This consortium will act as a State-wide school board that will oversee and administer Alaska Native schools and programs. (Twitchell, 2020)
Mandatory proficiency in Alaska Native language
To bring Alaska Native languages to commonplace in Alaska, this step involves instituting mandatory basic level proficiency in schools in order to graduate high-school and college. (Twitchell, 2020)
Let's Review!
Let's Review

Dr. X̱ʼunei Lance Twitchell has laid out 4 next-steps for the State of Alaska to take in order to see a more successful revitalization of Alaska Native languages and as part of the The Alaska Native Language Preservation & Advisory Council, sent these recommendations to the Alaskan Government in the 2022 Biennial Report to the Governor and Legislature. These steps include:

  1. Create a college for Alaska Native Languages within the University of Alaska.

  2. Re-shape the Board of Education in the State of Alaska to be in charge of Alaska Native language schools and programs.

  3. Create an Alaska Native school consortium within the Board of Education to be in charge of Alaska Native language schools and programs.

  4. Mandate proficiency in an Alaska Native Language inn schools.
